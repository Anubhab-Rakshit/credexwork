# Go-To-Market Strategy

## 1. The Exact Target User
We aren't targeting "startups." We are targeting the **"Default-Alive Engineer"** or the **"Fractional Head of Finance."**

*   **Primary Persona:** The "Founding Engineer" or "Head of Engineering" at a 12-25 person Seed/Series A AI-native startup. They are currently burning $2k-$8k/mo on AI tooling across Cursor, GitHub, Anthropic, and OpenAI. They have "billing fatigue"—they see the invoices but don't have 4 hours to cross-reference every plan.
*   **Secondary Persona:** The "Fractional CFO" for YC/Early-stage companies. They manage 5-10 different startups and want a "Value-Add" tool they can run for all their clients to immediately show ROI on their own consulting fee.

## 2. The "Pre-Audit" Behavior (Search & Scroll)
Right before they land on our tool, they are:
- Googling: *"Cursor Business vs Pro feature comparison Reddit"*
- Googling: *"Anthropic API credits bulk discount"*
- Searching on X: *"Is GPT-4o mini enough for RAG? cost comparison"*
- Scrolling through their **Quickbooks/Ramp dashboard** and clicking "Wait, why is this invoice 3x bigger than last month?"

## 3. Where They Hang Out
- **Specific Subreddits:** `r/ExperiencedDevs` (where they complain about tool sprawl) and `r/SaaS` (where they discuss margins).
- **Slack/Discord:** The `YC Bookface` (if they are YC), the `Elad Gil / AI` Discord server, and the `Lennys Newsletter` Slack (specifically the #engineering-and-product channel).
- **X Lists:** "AI Infrastructure Founders" or "CTOs of 2024 YC Batch."

## 4. First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
1.  **The "Invoice Roast" (Day 1-7):** Post on X and LinkedIn: *"I built a deterministic engine to roast your AI bills. DM me a screenshot of your tool list (hide the sensitive bits), and I'll send you a link to your audit. Free, no email required."* This creates a "Manual-to-Auto" loop.
2.  **The "YC Batch Audit" (Day 8-14):** Identify 50 companies in the latest YC/Techstars batches. Reach out to their CTOs with a *pre-filled* audit based on their publicly visible tech stack (e.g., "I see you're using OpenAI and GitHub Copilot—you could save $X by doing Y").
3.  **The Fractional CFO "Toolkit" (Day 15-21):** Cold DM 20 Fractional CFOs on LinkedIn. Offer them a "White-label" version (even if it's just a custom URL) they can send to their clients.
4.  **"Hacker News" Show HN (Day 22-30):** Launch with a focus on "Privacy-First Math"—emphasizing that we don't need their actual invoice files, just their tool counts. This lowers the trust barrier significantly.

## 5. The Unfair Distribution Channel
**The "Credex Ledger":** Credex already has a data pipeline of what "ideal" AI spend looks like for high-margin companies. We can generate a **"Benchmark Score"** (e.g., "Your spend is 40% higher than the Credex average for your headcount"). This creates a social proof loop that only Credex can facilitate because they own the "Target State" pricing data.

## 6. Week-1 Traction Looks Like...
If this works, by Day 7 we will see:
- **50+ Completed Audits.**
- **15+ "Shareable Link" views** (indicating the user sent it to their CEO/Finance person).
- **3 "Booked Consultation" clicks** (the primary conversion goal).
- **1 "Viral" Post** on X/LinkedIn with a screenshot of the "Savings Meter" (the "Aha" moment of the UI).

**What we won't do:** We will NOT focus on "SEO content marketing" or "Building a community." Those take 6 months. We are building a high-velocity lead magnet.
